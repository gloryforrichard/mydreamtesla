# Cybertruck by the Numbers: Every Spec You Need to Know

Love it or hate it, you can't ignore it. The Tesla Cybertruck looks like nothing else on the road, and the specs are just as wild as the design. Let's break down what this angular beast actually delivers — no fluff, just numbers.

## The Lineup at a Glance

Tesla currently offers the Cybertruck in three flavors:

- **Rear-Wheel Drive (Single Motor)** — the entry point
- **All-Wheel Drive (Dual Motor)** — the balanced option
- **Cyberbeast (Tri-Motor)** — the "why not" option

Each one is built on the same exoskeleton body, but the differences under the skin are significant.

## Range: How Far Can You Actually Go?

This is the question every EV buyer asks first.

- **RWD**: ~250 miles EPA
- **AWD**: ~318 miles EPA
- **Cyberbeast**: ~301 miles EPA

Want more? The **Range Extender** battery pack slots into the bed and pushes those numbers up considerably — the AWD with Range Extender hits an estimated **440+ miles**. It eats into your bed space, though, so it's a trade-off.

## Acceleration: Yes, It's Fast

This is a truck that weighs nearly 7,000 lbs. These numbers shouldn't be possible.

- **RWD**: 0-60 in ~6.5 seconds
- **AWD**: 0-60 in ~4.1 seconds
- **Cyberbeast**: 0-60 in **2.6 seconds** !!!

Read that last one again. A full-size pickup truck. 2.6 seconds. The Cyberbeast runs a quarter-mile in about 11 seconds flat. That's faster than most sports cars from five years ago.

## Towing & Payload

Here's where truck buyers actually care:

- **Towing capacity**: Up to **11,000 lbs** (Cyberbeast)
- **Payload**: Up to **2,500 lbs**
- **Bed length**: 6 feet with the tailgate up, extendable

For context, 11,000 lbs means you can tow a boat, a camper, or a horse trailer. The caveat: towing kills EV range, and the Cybertruck is no exception. Expect range to drop 40-50% when towing heavy loads.

## Dimensions: It's BIG

If you haven't seen one in person, you might not realize how massive this thing is.

- **Length**: 223.7 inches (18.6 feet)
- **Width**: 79.8 inches (without mirrors)
- **Height**: 70.5 inches
- **Ground clearance**: Up to 17.1 inches with air suspension fully raised

For comparison, it's about 5 inches longer than a Ford F-150 and sits noticeably taller. Parking garages are... a challenge.

## The Exoskeleton: 30X Cold-Rolled Stainless Steel

This is the spec that makes the Cybertruck truly unique. Instead of a traditional body-on-frame design with painted steel panels, the Cybertruck uses an **ultra-hard 30X cold-rolled stainless steel exoskeleton** that *is* the structure.

What this means in practice:

- No paint. The body is raw brushed stainless steel (wraps are available for color).
- Extremely dent-resistant (but not scratch-proof — fine scratches are common)
- Corrosion-resistant
- Stops certain calibers of bullets (yes, Tesla actually tested this)

The flat panels aren't just for looks — curved stainless steel this thick would be nearly impossible to stamp. The angular design is engineering dictating aesthetics.

## Suspension & Off-Road Capability

Every Cybertruck comes with **adaptive air suspension** as standard:

- **4 ride height settings** ranging from Entry (for getting in) to Extract (maximum height for off-road)
- Up to **17.1 inches of ground clearance** in the highest setting
- **Approach angle**: 35.0 degrees
- **Departure angle**: 28.0 degrees
- **Up to 2.5 feet of wading depth**

The AWD and Cyberbeast models get front and rear locking differentials for serious off-road use.

## Tech Inside

- **18.5-inch center touchscreen** — the biggest in any Tesla
- **9.4-inch rear passenger display**
- Rear-seat wireless charging
- 120V and 240V power outlets in the bed (vehicle-to-load)
- **Up to 9.6 kW of power output** — enough to power tools, appliances, or even charge another EV
- Powered tonneau cover
- Powered frunk

## Price

Starting MSRPs (as of current pricing):

- **RWD**: ~$59,990
- **AWD**: ~$79,990
- **Cyberbeast**: ~$99,990

The Range Extender and Foundation Series packages push these higher. After adding FSD and other options, a fully loaded Cyberbeast crosses $120,000.
