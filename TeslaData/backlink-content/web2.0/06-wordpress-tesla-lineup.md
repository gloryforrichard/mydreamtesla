# Tesla's 2025 Model Lineup: Everything You Need to Know

Tesla's 2025 lineup represents the most significant refresh cycle in the company's history. Two of its five models received ground-up redesigns, pricing has shifted across the board, and the federal tax credit makes the entry-level Teslas more affordable than ever. Whether you're a first-time EV buyer or upgrading from an older Tesla, this guide covers every model, every trim, and every key spec you need to know.

All specifications in this guide are sourced from [MyDreamTesla.com](https://mydreamtesla.com), which maintains a comprehensive database of every Tesla trim across all model years with 50+ specs per vehicle.

## Tesla Model 3 (2025 Highland)

**Starting Price:** $38,990 | **Body Type:** Compact Sedan | **In Production Since:** 2017

The Model 3 is Tesla's volume leader and the best-selling electric car in history. The 2024-2025 "Highland" generation brought the most substantial update since the car's 2017 debut, with a completely redesigned exterior, overhauled interior, and improved driving dynamics.

### What Changed with Highland

The Highland redesign (introduced late 2023 for 2024 models, continuing into 2025) transformed the Model 3's look and feel:

- **Exterior:** New front fascia without the traditional "nose cone," full-width LED light bars front and rear, smoother body panels with improved aerodynamics
- **Interior:** Redesigned dashboard with ambient lighting, new materials on doors and dash, ventilated front seats standard on all trims, rear passenger entertainment screen
- **Performance:** Updated Autopilot hardware with a new AI chip, improved NVH (noise, vibration, harshness) insulation, 11.5 kW onboard charger across all trims
- **Range:** The RWD trim improved to 321 miles (up from 272 on the pre-Highland base), while the Premium RWD reaches 363 miles

### 2025 Model 3 Trims

| Trim | Drive | MSRP | Range | 0-60 | HP | Tax Credit |
|------|-------|------|-------|------|----|------------|
| RWD | RWD | $38,990 | 321 mi | 5.8s | 272 | $7,500 |
| Premium RWD | RWD | $41,990 | 363 mi | 4.9s | ~310 | $7,500 |
| Long Range AWD | AWD | $42,990 | 346 mi | 4.2s | ~350 | $7,500 |
| Performance | AWD | $54,990 | 309 mi | 2.9s | 510 | $7,500 |

**Best Value Pick:** The RWD at $31,490 after the federal tax credit. At that price point, you get 321 miles of range, a 15.4-inch touchscreen, Autopilot, and the full Highland interior. No other EV at this price comes close.

**Enthusiast Pick:** The Performance at $54,990. With 510 hp, 2.9-second 0-60, and Track Mode 3.0, this is one of the fastest sedans money can buy at any price point.

## Tesla Model Y (2025 Juniper)

**Starting Price:** $44,990 | **Body Type:** Compact SUV | **In Production Since:** 2020

The Model Y is the world's best-selling electric vehicle and Tesla's commercial backbone. The 2025 "Juniper" refresh follows the Highland playbook — new exterior, upgraded interior, improved NVH — making the most popular EV on Earth even better.

### What Changed with Juniper

- **Exterior:** Completely redesigned front and rear fascia with full-width light bars, new wheel designs, improved aerodynamics
- **Interior:** Rear passenger entertainment screen, ventilated front seats, new ambient lighting package, upgraded cabin materials
- **Ride Quality:** Retuned suspension, improved sound insulation, more refined highway cruising experience
- **Tech:** Updated Autopilot hardware with the latest AI processing chip

### 2025 Model Y Trims

| Trim | Drive | MSRP | Range | 0-60 | HP | Towing |
|------|-------|------|-------|------|----|--------|
| RWD | RWD | $44,990 | 321 mi | 6.8s | 260 | — |
| AWD | AWD | $47,990 | 294 mi | 4.6s | ~350 | 3,500 lb |
| Premium RWD | RWD | $46,990 | 357 mi | 5.4s | ~310 | — |
| Long Range AWD | AWD | $49,990 | 327 mi | 4.6s | ~350 | 3,500 lb |
| Performance | AWD | $55,990 | 306 mi | 3.3s | 510 | 3,500 lb |

**Best Value Pick:** The RWD at $37,490 after the $7,500 federal tax credit. You get the full Juniper experience — new design, rear screen, ventilated seats — at a very competitive price for a midsize SUV.

**Family Pick:** The Long Range AWD at $49,990. With 327 miles of range, all-wheel drive for winter confidence, and 3,500 lb towing capacity, this is the trim that does everything well.

**Who Should Consider the Model Y Over the Model 3:** Anyone who needs more cargo space (76 cu ft vs. 23 cu ft), prefers a higher seating position, wants towing capability, or has a family with multiple car seats. The $6,000 premium over the equivalent Model 3 trim is well worth it for buyers who need the versatility.

## Tesla Model S (2025)

**Starting Price:** ~$74,990 | **Body Type:** Full-Size Luxury Sedan | **In Production Since:** 2012

The Model S is Tesla's flagship sedan — the car that proved electric vehicles could be desirable, fast, and long-range. While it hasn't received a Highland-style redesign recently, the current generation (updated in 2021 with the Plaid powertrain and new interior) remains one of the most impressive EVs on sale.

### 2025 Model S Trims

| Trim | Drive | MSRP | Range | 0-60 | HP | Key Feature |
|------|-------|------|-------|------|----|-------------|
| AWD | Dual Motor | ~$74,990 | 402 mi | 3.1s | 670 | Longest range of any Tesla |
| Plaid | Tri-Motor | ~$89,990 | 359 mi | 1.99s | 1,020 | Fastest sedan on Earth |

### Why Consider the Model S

- **Maximum Range:** At 402 miles EPA, the Model S AWD has the longest range of any Tesla — and one of the longest of any production EV. For drivers who regularly make long trips or live far from Superchargers, this margin matters.
- **Luxury Interior:** The 17-inch landscape touchscreen, 22-speaker audio system, and rear entertainment screens create an interior experience that justifies the premium over the Model 3.
- **Plaid Performance:** The 1.99-second 0-60 time makes the Model S Plaid the quickest production car ever made. It's faster than a Bugatti Chiron to 60 mph — at a fraction of the price.

**Who It's For:** Buyers who want Tesla's best range, best interior, or best performance in a sedan. It competes with the Mercedes EQS, Porsche Taycan Turbo S, and BMW i7 — and leads on range and acceleration. ## Tesla Model X (2025)

**Starting Price:** ~$79,990 | **Body Type:** Full-Size Luxury SUV | **In Production Since:** 2015

The Model X remains Tesla's most distinctive vehicle, defined by its iconic falcon-wing rear doors that open upward — even in tight parking spaces. It's the Tesla for families who need space, luxury, and performance in one package.

### 2025 Model X Trims

| Trim | Drive | MSRP | Range | 0-60 | HP | Seating |
|------|-------|------|-------|------|----|---------|
| AWD | Dual Motor | ~$79,990 | 335 mi | 3.8s | 670 | Up to 7 |
| Plaid | Tri-Motor | ~$94,990 | 311 mi | 2.5s | 1,020 | Up to 7 |

### The Falcon-Wing Advantage

The Model X's falcon-wing doors are more than aesthetics. They:

- Open vertically, requiring less lateral clearance than sliding doors or traditional SUV doors
- Provide unobstructed access to the second and third rows, making car seat installation dramatically easier
- Include sensors that detect obstacles above and to the sides, adjusting their opening height accordingly

For parents juggling multiple children in car seats, this feature alone can justify the Model X over a Model Y.

### Model X vs. Model Y

The Model X costs roughly $35,000 more than a comparable Model Y trim. What do you get for that premium?

- **True three-row seating** (the Model Y's optional third row is small)
- **Falcon-wing doors** for easier passenger access
- **More cargo space** (91 cu ft vs. 76 cu ft)
- **5,000 lb towing capacity** (vs. 3,500 lb for the Y)
- **More powerful base powertrain** (670 hp vs. ~350 hp)
- **Premium interior** with 17-inch screen and 22-speaker audio

**Who It's For:** Large families (3+ children), buyers who need genuine third-row space, anyone who prioritizes luxury and is willing to pay for it, and Plaid enthusiasts who want the fastest SUV available.

## Tesla Cybertruck (2025)

**Starting Price:** $69,990 | **Body Type:** Electric Pickup Truck | **In Production Since:** 2023

The Cybertruck is the most polarizing vehicle Tesla has ever made — and possibly the most polarizing vehicle any manufacturer has ever made. Its angular stainless-steel exoskeleton, armored glass, and post-apocalyptic aesthetic are a deliberate departure from every design convention in the auto industry.

But beyond the design, the Cybertruck is a genuinely capable truck.

### 2025 Cybertruck Trims

| Trim | Drive | MSRP | Range | 0-60 | HP | Towing |
|------|-------|------|-------|------|----|--------|
| Long Range RWD | RWD | $69,990 | 325+ mi | ~6.5s | ~315 | 7,500 lb |
| AWD | Dual Motor | $79,990 | 325 mi | 4.3s | 600 | 11,000 lb |
| Cyberbeast | Tri-Motor | $99,990 | 320 mi | 2.6s | 845 | 11,000 lb |

### What Makes the Cybertruck Different

- **Stainless Steel Exoskeleton:** The body panels are structural — there's no traditional body-on-frame construction. The ultra-hard 30X cold-rolled stainless steel resists dents and corrosion without paint.
- **800V Architecture:** The Cybertruck uses an 800-volt electrical system (most other Teslas use 400V), enabling faster DC charging at V4 Superchargers up to 325 kW.
- **Adaptive Air Suspension:** Standard on all trims, with adjustable ride height from load-leveling low to maximum off-road clearance.
- **6-Foot Bed with Tonneau Cover:** The powered tonneau cover is integrated into the design, providing secure, weatherproof cargo storage.

### Foundation Series vs. Standard

The 2024 Cybertrucks were exclusively "Foundation Series" models — limited-edition trims with FSD (Full Self-Driving) included, Powershare bidirectional charging, and unique badging. The 2025 models are standard trims at significantly lower prices. The Cyberbeast dropped from $119,990 (Foundation) to $99,990 (standard) — a $20,000 reduction.

### Who It's For

The Cybertruck is for buyers who:
- Need a full-size truck with 11,000 lb towing capacity
- Want to go electric without compromising on truck capability
- Embrace the unconventional design (this is non-negotiable — you either love it or you don't)
- Want the fastest pickup truck ever made (Cyberbeast's 2.6s 0-60)

For detailed Cybertruck specs including dimensions, payload, and bed size, visit the [Cybertruck page on MyDreamTesla](https://mydreamtesla.com).

## The Bottom Line

Tesla's 2025 lineup is the strongest and most diverse it has ever been:

- **Entry-level buyers** can get a Model 3 for $31,490 after credits — an exceptional deal for a 321-mile EV with premium tech.
- **Families** have the Model Y Juniper, which is now better than ever with the refresh, starting at $37,490 after credits.
- **Luxury buyers** have the Model S and Model X, which deliver range, performance, and interior quality that compete with six-figure European sedans and SUVs.
- **Truck buyers** have the Cybertruck, which offers capabilities no other electric truck matches at these price points.

The federal tax credit on Model 3 and Model Y makes 2025 a particularly good time to buy. The Highland and Juniper redesigns mean you're getting the best versions of Tesla's most popular cars. And the Supercharger network — still the largest and most reliable fast-charging network in North America — makes long-distance EV ownership practical in a way that no competitor has matched.

Do your research, compare the specs that matter to you, and take a test drive. The right Tesla is out there — it just depends on what you need it to do.
