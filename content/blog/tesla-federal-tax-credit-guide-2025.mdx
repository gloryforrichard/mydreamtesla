## The Federal EV Tax Credit Explained

The Inflation Reduction Act (IRA) provides a **federal tax credit of up to $7,500** for qualifying new electric vehicles purchased in the United States. For Tesla buyers, this credit can significantly reduce the effective price of a new vehicle.

As of 2025, the credit can be applied **at the point of sale** — meaning you see the savings immediately at the dealership or on Tesla's website, rather than waiting to file your taxes.

## Which Teslas Qualify?

Eligibility depends on the vehicle's MSRP, battery sourcing requirements, and final assembly location. As of early 2025:

| Model | Base MSRP | Max Credit | Effective Price |
|-------|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| [Model 3](/models/model-3) | ~$38,990 | $7,500 | ~$31,490 |
| [Model Y](/models/model-y) | ~$44,990 | $7,500 | ~$37,490 |
| [Model S](/models/model-s) | ~$74,990 | Varies | Check eligibility |
| [Model X](/models/model-x) | ~$79,990 | Varies | Check eligibility |

**Important**: The Model 3 and Model Y generally qualify for the full $7,500 credit. The Model S and Model X may qualify for a partial credit depending on battery component sourcing, which changes periodically.

## MSRP Limits

The IRA sets maximum MSRP thresholds:
- **Sedans**: $55,000 (applies to Model 3, Model S)
- **SUVs / Trucks**: $80,000 (applies to Model Y, Model X, Cybertruck)

If the vehicle's MSRP exceeds these limits, it does not qualify — even if the trim is otherwise eligible.

## Income Limits

Your modified adjusted gross income (MAGI) must be below:
- **$300,000** for married filing jointly
- **$225,000** for head of household
- **$150,000** for single filers

You can use either the current year's or prior year's income — whichever qualifies.

## How to Claim the Credit

### At Point of Sale (Recommended)
Since January 2024, you can transfer the credit directly to the dealer/Tesla at purchase. This means:
1. You see the $7,500 discount immediately
2. No need to wait for tax filing season
3. Tesla handles the paperwork

### On Your Tax Return
Alternatively, you can claim the credit when filing your federal taxes using **IRS Form 8936**. This makes sense if:
- You're unsure about income eligibility at purchase time
- You prefer to handle it yourself

## Used Tesla Credit

If you're buying a **used** Tesla, a separate credit of up to **$4,000** is available:
- Vehicle must be at least 2 model years old
- Sale price must be $25,000 or less
- Same income limits apply (lower thresholds)

## Tips for Maximizing Savings

1. **Stack credits**: Combine the federal credit with state and local incentives. Many states offer additional $2,000-$5,000.
2. **Check eligibility quarterly**: Battery sourcing rules change, affecting which trims qualify.
3. **Consider timing**: If your income fluctuates, purchase in a year when your MAGI qualifies.
4. **Model Y classification**: The Model Y is classified as an SUV, giving it the higher $80,000 MSRP cap.

## Bottom Line

For most buyers, the **Model 3 and Model Y offer the best value** after applying the full $7,500 credit — bringing the Model 3 below $32,000 and the Model Y below $38,000.
